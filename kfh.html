The retail app from the bank worth $101 billion required me to be a project manager, designer and design lead for one and a half years.
BRIEF

The first completely digital personal finance app from KFH Bank in Bahrain needed to extend the feature set along with design overhaul. I lead design from start to finish and most of this case study will also discuss the process improvements which happened as we moved further.




It started as a website redesign project for KFH Jazeel and I proposed some. They liked the approach we suggested and asked if I could apply similar visual language to a part of their app. Slowly it turned into a full blown project of redesigning their app which went through multiple iterations both on design and development side.

KFH Jazeel’s brand has two main values; taking care of Community & being 100% digital.

The first module

KFH Jazeel wanted to be more than a place where people store money and do transactions and they wanted to become a bigger part of Bahrain’s social circle.

Loyalty app was a manifestation of the above goals. I got some rudimentry wireframes from the bank's team and followed the native iOS design as per the guidelines.

While I was designing this app, I was also reading a bit on design systems. I was already fluent in Sketch’s symbols and how to utilize their power in UI design but I hadn’t practiced creating a proper design system from scratch. Sketch had introduced a lot of features to faciliate design system and since this app had quite a lot of elements, I figured that I could atleast practice following atomic design methodology in the symbols that were being created. I got a brand guideline from the bank earlier which I converted into color and type styles:

The app ended up being extensive enough that I had a good amount of components collected. I used and refined these components later on when I was designing retail app.

Emails, Calls, Notes, Threads, Chats & CCing

During the work, the bank's team asked for some design directions for retail app as well. We presented some and due to extended functionality concerns, they decided to keep the design of the app as minimal and functional as possible. It also needed to be able to developed quickly. We opted for a hybrid model where the design will follow the native environment as much as possible.

I also started to realize that our processes of working weren't suited for a large scale and fast paced project like this. We were communicating through emails and the threads were getting longer and longer. Taking notes of design changes on the calls and email and then synching and verifying them became a hassle.

Working Smartly

I discussed the matter and introduced an alternate solution which was gradually adopted for all the design reviews and feedbacks.

The new process took us away from long chain of email threads and was much more efficient for managing communication and feedback loops.

Designing Smartly

Our internal design process also required updation as we needed tight collaboration and work with even tighter deadlines.

I introduced an alternate workflow which took collaboration away from XD+Email+Slack and towards Sketch+Invision+Abstract.

Abstract took care of the versioning. Every designer could have the updated files on any part of the project regardless of who is working on it. We were also aware of what the other person was working on and I could verify the work without interrupting their flow.

None of the other designers had to explain the changes as well as Abstract had a clean way of presenting all the changes that have happened in the file in every commit.

The Cost of Switching

It worth noting that we did ponder over versioning with XD using google drive but ultimately decided that it was best to simply switch. The cost of switching at the time was around 5K to 6K USD. I contacted a dealer and got the macs for the product team and then came the hard part: implementing and getting everyone onboard!

Making ROI Positive; Design System!

While we were excited about the new hardware and softwares. I mean it when I say that we pushed the throttle to the max when I shifted everyone to the design system. Remember Loyalty app from earlier? I took the components I made earlier and created a small prototype of design system which we started using. I taught the team how to use a design system to generate screens and how to update and extend it. We were making full use of abstract while doing this:

At first, I monitored actively when designers interacted with design system since I didn't want it to mess up while we were pushing deliveries and it would have been way too much of a risk. But I slowly let the reigns go loose and we were flying like a unicorn 🦄. The designers even implemented the arabic version of design system on their own with minimal help from my side.
